Our Process

How to Place Your Order

01

Contact us through our online form or call us directly to discuss your needs. Our gunsmiths have years of experience and are happy to answer any questions or concerns you may have.

02

Receive a detailed quote and estimated completion time. We pride ourselves on our attention to detail and our fast turn-around times.

03

Ship your barrel following our detailed preparation instructions, or bring your barrel directly to our shop.

How to Ship Your Barrel

Disassembly

Remove the trigger, optics, lower, and any accessories including rings, lights, bipods, slings, etc. from the upper/barrel. We don't want your entire firearm, just the barrel.

Packaging

Wrap the barrel in protective materials and place in a sturdy container. If your barrel/upper fits in a 4" x 8" x 48" box then it should fit in our cnc machine easily. If it's larger than that contact us before shipping.

Shipping

Use a tracked shipping service and insure the package for its full value. When the threading is completed we will contact you and you can pick up your barrel at our shop or we will mail it back to you.

Frequently Asked Questions

The correct thread pitch depends on your caliber and muzzle diameter. Rimfire and .20–.24 calibers typically use 1/2x28 or 7/16x28. Calibers from .243–.358 typically use 5/8x24 or 1/2x28. Larger calibers (.375–.458) typically use 3/4x24 or 5/8x24. Our quote form will guide you through the options for your specific caliber.

Most barrel threading jobs are completed within a few business days of receiving your barrel. We will contact you as soon as the work is done so you can arrange pickup or return shipping.

The minimum muzzle diameter depends on the thread pitch. For 1/2x28 and 7/16x28, the minimum is .560". For 5/8x24, the minimum is .685". For 3/4x24, the minimum is .810". If your barrel does not meet the minimum diameter for your desired thread pitch, we will let you know before any work is performed.
